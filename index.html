Scene-conditioned Foley Sound Synthesis via Schr√∂dinger Bridge Conditional Flow Matching

Abstract

We propose SB-CFM, a scene-conditioned Foley sound synthesis method based on Schr√∂dinger Bridge Conditional Flow Matching. Unlike diffusion models that rely on iterative denoising, SB-CFM directly learns a velocity field that transports noise to realistic audio via entropic optimal transport coupling and Schr√∂dinger bridge regularization. This formulation is particularly suited for Foley synthesis, where each scene category exhibits significant acoustic diversity and multiple plausible realizations exist for a single condition. We adopt SDE-based sampling combined with classifier-free guidance to balance conditional fidelity and generation diversity. On the DCASE 2023 Task 7 dataset, SB-CFM achieves state-of-the-art performance across all evaluation metrics (FAD 6.02, Acc 0.94, KL 0.05, IS 5.18), demonstrating that flow matching can be a competitive alternative for conditional audio generation.

Method Overview

Key Features

Schr√∂dinger Bridge Formulation: We leverage entropic optimal transport to couple noise and data samples, learning the drift of Brownian bridges rather than deterministic ODE paths. This approach retains the benefits of optimal transport while introducing probabilistic flexibility, crucial for modeling multi-modal Foley distributions.

UNet2D Velocity Network: A 2D UNet architecture directly models mel-spectrograms with 45M parameters. Time and class conditioning are injected through separate pathways‚Äîsinusoidal time embeddings with adaptive group normalization, and class embeddings via FiLM layers‚Äîto prevent interference between temporal and semantic information.

SDE Sampling with Classifier-Free Guidance: During inference, we use SDE-based Euler-Maruyama sampling (2000 steps) combined with classifier-free guidance (CFG scale 3.0) to achieve optimal balance between conditional fidelity and diversity. This addresses the inherently one-to-many nature of Foley synthesis without requiring external classifiers.
